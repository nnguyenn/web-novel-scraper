A few more days passed.

<Master.>

Yurnet’s voice tickled my ears.

It was late evening. I was organizing documents at my desk.

<I apologize if you were sleeping.>

“I was doing some chores.”

<It seems we haven’t been in touch much lately…>

“I’ve had quite a lot to do.”

I had been postponing matters related to Niflheim.

This was the first communication with Yurnet since sending Eolka away.

I put the documents in the drawer and locked it.

“What’s up? You wouldn’t call just to say hi.”

<I wanted to bid you goodnight.>

“That’s a funny joke.”

<…Didn’t you say you’d contact me every week?>

“Hmm?”

<Nothing. Just had something in my throat.>

Yurnet coughed and then continued.

“I have something to report to the Master. It’s a significant issue, so I’ve determined it cannot be decided upon unilaterally.”

“A significant issue, huh.”

I leaned back in my chair.

Something that even Yurnet, the current sub-master, couldn’t decide on.

There was only one thing that came to mind.

“Is it about the Union Guild?”

<Indeed, Master. You’ve hit the nail on the head.>

“Save the buttered-up compliments. If there’s something to tell, then tell it.”

<There are rumors that the Union Guild’s forces are gathering in one place.>

“And the investigation results?”

“It’s true. Hundreds of airships are gathering from across the server.”

I smirked.

There weren’t any world raids happening.

Yet, they were gathering forces now, out of the blue.

‘Obvious enough.’

<Master, these bastards… I mean, a scheme by members of the Union Guild has been confirmed.>

“I see.”

Among the comments on a video uploaded by Anytng, there was something that stood out.

It was about recruiting reserve members for the Union Guild.

It had been left unchecked amidst indifference.

<It’s a bit different.>

“Different how?”

<Take a look.>

A window popped up in my view.

A play button on a red rectangle. The icon of YouTube.

But the background was slightly different. This is…

‘My channel, isn’t it.’

It was Loki’s channel.

Boasting about five million subscribers.

Do I really need to be in Niflheimr?

Not really. Niflheimr’s operational system was already complete.

But, there’s never been such a case, so who knows.

I’ve always been waging wars with missions.

I stroked my chin.

The goal of the Union Guild was clear enough without having to see it.

According to reports, they hadn’t touched any other rankers.

Just me.

Faster than I expected.

‘What could a low-level do in a high-level fight zone.’

<I’ve sent a dispatch request to the Master of Townia. They’ll understand. The airship will arrive in two days. Please take care of yourself until then.>

“Call back everyone. Don’t let anyone get killed for no reason. And it would be good to have detailed power and combat data on them.”

<Thank you for granting my request, Master.>

I had promised to help.

I was just joking. There was no way I could refuse.

‘Maybe now is the best time.’

A rest period to replenish forces.

It was okay to be away for a while.

I looked at the ring on my left hand.

A black sheen flowed along the surface of the ring.

Yurnet had said that this, a gift from her, was proof of being the owner of Niflheimr.

I guess I should repay the favor received.

‘Is it war then.’

Niflheimr never runs from a fight.

We have always fought head-on.

Whoever it is.

There were numerous challenges,

A very large guild with hundreds of members, and then it was a guild alliance from another server, even a ranker like me tried to conquer Niflheimr, but no one succeeded.

‘129 wins out of 129.’

This was the 130th time.

I got up from my seat.

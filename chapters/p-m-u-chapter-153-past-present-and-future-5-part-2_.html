[Master, 587 unidentified channel chats have been detected.]

[Would you like to check?]

[Yes / No (selected)]

I ignored it.

There’s nothing to discuss with those about to die.

I spoke up.

“How’s the situation with their other forces?”

“Myuden is suppressing them along with the 1st fleet.”

“Good.”

I crossed my arms.

The Union Guild’s total power was about 1,000 airships.

They deployed about 600 of them here, while the rest went to attack Niflheimr’s main base. There was no need to investigate. It was expected.

‘A decoy from the start.’

What we brought to the battlefield was merely a small airship.

We couldn’t sacrifice the latest airships for this battle.

[‘Union Guild’ member ‘Rodika’ has declared surrender!]

[Do you accept?]

[Yes / No (selected)]

[‘Union Guild’ member ‘Kilyud’ has…]

[‘Union Guild’ member…]

I rejected all the surrender declarations in succession.

I leaned deeply into the back of my chair. The clear sky was now colored red with the glow and flash from the burning airships.

“You bastards…!”

Flash.

A light appeared in a corner of the bridge, and several heroes appeared.

It was clear at a glance. They were the Union Guild’s key figures, each with a twisted expression.

“We will erase you from this world, even if it costs us our lives!”

A knight in white armor, wielding a shining sword, charged at me.

Before he could take a few steps, he was split into dozens of pieces and scattered.

The woman behind him gritted her teeth.

“You won’t even accept a surrender!”

“At least the innocent crew members…”

“Ridgion.”

Snikt.

As if they never existed, they disappeared.

I cooled my gaze.

“Not a single one of them should be spared.”

“As you command.”

The airships attempting to leave the area returned to their places as if nothing had happened.

Their piloting functions had been taken away.

‘I can’t forgive backstabbers.’

They said they would settle everything in one place but went to attack the main base on the other side.

They had sold their consciences.

[What are you…?]

Iselle looked around at Yurnet.

There was fear in her gaze.

Yurnet smiled.

“If you want to stop of being an intern, get used to it.”

[To what?]

“Winning.”

Anyway.

I moved my hand to the control window on the left side of my sight.

It was Yurnet’s doing. The master’s screen was displayed.

I entered the hero tab and adjusted the fleet.

The target was the bases of Union Guild members we had identified in advance.

Not a single blade of grass would be left. The plan was to uproot them completely.

‘Millions will fly away in minutes.’

Well, it’s not my money.

I ordered the advance.

Considering everything, there were hundreds of airships and thousands of men.

They had used so much, yet survivors remained.

However, they had lost all their combat capabilities.

They wouldn’t be able to resist.

‘It’s about time they showed up.’

I rested my chin in my hand.

There were those who hadn’t appeared until now.

A particularly notable group among the Union Guild’s heroes.

“Master, it’s them.”

Yurnet whispered in my ear.

I nodded. They are quite late.

They should have incurred enough losses by now.

Swoosh.

In the left corner of the bridge, dark shadows gathered.

Shadows that appeared with an ominous aura rose up, each taking the form of a person.

Three individuals, two men, and one woman, their profiles popped up in my sight.

[Uruka Dije Lv.99]

[Siha Niatra Lv.99]

[Nord Lv.?]

A robust old man, a woman with a bursting figure, and a young man with a pallid expression like a corpse.

Each had a distinctive appearance.

The commander of the airship opened his mouth.

“Those are…!”

“Who are they?”

“They are direct heroes under the Union Guild’s leader! Be careful. They are on a different level from the heroes we’ve faced so far!”

“Why are you speaking as if you’re on our side? You’re an enemy.”

Realizing his mistake, the commander had a dumbfounded expression.

The woman looked at the commander with a flirtatious gaze.

“Commander, you were trusted with this position by the leader, and you betray us like this?”

“No, I… argh!”

Suddenly, black blood flowed from the captain’s eyes, nose, and ears, and soon he collapsed onto the floor.

Then, after twitching, his movements stopped. He was dead.

“Ha, what a death befitting a bug.”

The woman’s gaze turned towards me.

“Oh my, a handsome man. Won’t you play with me?”

“……”

The old man burst into laughter and stepped forward.

“Ahaha! Welcome, warriors of Niflheimr. I am Uruka Dije, an advisor of the Union Guild. I look forward to working with you!”

“I’m Siha Niatra, in charge of beauty in the Union Guild. Nice to meet you.”

Siha winked at me.

The young man with the pale complexion said nothing.

“You might think you’ve won against us, but don’t be mistaken. These are just a small part of our forces. They’re as insignificant as bugs.”

Siha swayed her large chest as she walked forward.

“Uhuhu, I am a witch cursed with seven plagues, an incarnation of immortality. An eternal witch who is not permitted to die. I shall grant you a painful and beautiful death, the absolute…”

Crack!

Lightning struck, and Siha’s upper body disappeared.

Her lower body, left behind, trembled and then collapsed to the side.

“Oops, shot her by mistake.”

Nihaku, having returned at some point, smiled sheepishly.

Uruka smiled warmly.

“Don’t worry. She won’t die from this. Come on, get up, we have guests waiting.”

“……”

Twitch, twitch.

The legs that showcased her figure trembled and then slumped.

And then they moved no more.

“……Why aren’t you getting up?”

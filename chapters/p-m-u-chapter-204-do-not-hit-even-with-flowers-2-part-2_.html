And the next afternoon.

“Slowly! Come out slowly!”

From the hangar installed on the left side of the Dimensional Rift, the airship was moving.

Radi, dressed in a yellow maintenance suit, was waving a signal stick, guiding the newly constructed airship, Lucette, towards the exit.

“Don’t be nervous. Breathe comfortably. Relax your hands!”

<Yes, sir!>

Bang!

The left wing of the Lucette hit the corner of the hangar and crumpled.

“…Is it really safe?”

Jenna looked at me anxiously.

Velkist, with two swords on his back, had his eyes closed.

“It will be safe. Probably.”

“Probably? I hate the idea of dying in a crash.”

Bang.

With a loud noise, the Lucette came to a halt.

A small airship, two levels smaller than Capitalism.

Since we were going out in small numbers, this would be sufficient.

‘It’s gotten noisy here too.’

It was completely silent when it was first being made.

I looked to the right. Under Katiio’s command, the third airship was being built.

Dozens of technicians were assembling the basic frame of the airship with wood and metal.

<You can board now!>

Clank. Clank.

The stairs descended from the railing of the Lucette.

I firmly gripped my spare sword sheath and stepped on the first step of the stairs.

“Take care of the waiting room while I’m away.”

“Don’t worry.”

Neryssa bowed politely.

Beside her, Kishasha waved her hand.

“Have a good trip, Han. I wanted to go too, but Goo Goo Con keeps begging me to play with him…”

<This little brat… I want to go too…!>

“No, Goo Goo! Stay still!”

“Guoo!”

As Kishasha strengthened her grip, the pigeon dangled from her fingertips.

I laughed lightly and finished climbing the stairs.

<Traitor! After I saved you, you abandoned me here!>

Noisy.

Someone seemed to be nagging, but I decided to ignore it.

When Jenna finally boarded, the stairs went up and folded.

<Lucette departing! Hold on tight!>

Wooong.

The Lucette, half-lifted from the ground, began moving toward the dimensional vortex.

Kishasha, holding the wriggling Goo Goo in her arms, exited the Dimensional Rift.

Neryssa was watching me from inside the railing.

‘Get ready.’

I whispered to Neryssa.

She nodded lightly.

5-star summon.

With a big fight ahead, there was no time to delay.

After the occupation battle, we planned to start immediately upon returning to the waiting room.

As the airship exited the tower, I gathered Jenna and Velkist in the cabin to begin the briefing for the occupation battle.

When the fight begins, tens to hundreds of people will engage in a chaotic battle on the Ruin field.

“We need a formation, right? It’s a triangle formation, isn’t it?”

“Yes. Each of us has to handle a sector. Didn’t we practice this to death before?”

“Recently, I’ve been training alone a lot.”

“Well, that’s fine. There will be constant fighting, I mean.”

Clank!

The airship swayed sharply to the right.

Velkist frowned.

“Terrible piloting.”

“She’s a novice.”

The current pilot of the Lucette was Radi’s assistant.

His current position was assistant mechanic. Since Radi couldn’t leave the waiting room due to airship construction, we had to bring along such a guy for navigation.

“Don’t let your guard down just because we have time. Even approaching the Ruin could get us attacked.”

Almost all Ruins had small or large battles.

Whether skirmishes or full-scale wars.

Even briefly occupying it could yield great rewards.

‘It’s about time.’

It had been several hours since the Lucette departed.

It would take 30 to 40 minutes at most to reach the Ruin.

From my memory, this was a chaotic area where battles occurred simultaneously.

<Hey!>

A hurried voice echoed through the ship’s speaker.

I listened closely to the broadcast.

<We have spotted an approaching airship ahead!>

Jenna and Velkist’s expressions changed.

I signaled to the two and went out to the deck.

Half-drawing my sword from its sheath.

When I reached the deck, a white dot was approaching from beyond the sky.

An airship from another faction.

As I squinted, I could roughly see its outline.

‘A small, no, an ultra-small one?’

The airship was getting closer.

Ready for battle, I had to let go of my sword’s handle.

‘What is that.’

Rumble.

A tiny boat, like a fishing boat, was flying toward us.

It didn’t have a cannon or even a ballista, and only one shabby-dressed woman was standing on its deck. In the corner, items that looked like supplies were piled up.

“…”

The woman’s eyes met mine.

She glanced at me indifferently and then turned her gaze away.

The ultra-small airship passed by the Lucette.

‘What is that.’

It was definitely headed in the direction of the Ruin.

On a battlefield, such an unarmed airship flying peacefully here?

Moreover, in the corner of the boat, there were materials presumed to be loot.

<Uh, well…>

“Ignore it and keep going.”

The short girl in the cockpit nodded.

‘…No way.’

Several uneasy premonitions surfaced.

That feeling intensified as we approached the Ruin.

All the airships we encountered on the way were unarmed merchant ships.

Even though they must have seen us, they passed by without any combat posture.

Some even greeted us.

“Didn’t you say there would be constant fighting?”

Velkist looked back at me.

The sky was clear, and airships were floating in the sky.

There was no sound of cannon fire anywhere.

‘Is it already solidly occupied?’

Even so, there were no surveillance ships from other factions.

In Niflheimr’s territory, surveillance airships could be found frequently.

Finally, the Lucette approached right in front of the Ruin.

A huge island floating in the sky. On top of it, a giant crystal dozens of meters in size was standing.

It was the Ruin field’s scenery.

However, there were no high walls, no tight cannon networks, and no airships preparing for bombardment. Merchant ships were just coming and going along the harbor.

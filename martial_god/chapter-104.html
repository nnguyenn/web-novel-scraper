The otherworldly Undead Legion, Kill the King.

They held more than half the responsibility for Earth’s doom.

‘If the World Tree Alliance was responsible for Earth’s continuous losses in the Space League… Kill the King was in charge of the clean-up.’

On planets that rank low in the Space League, more dungeons appear.

The World Tree Alliance had decisively crushed Earth in the league matches.

And from the dungeons that subsequently opened, Undead Legions with banners bearing a golden crown pierced by three spears poured out.

‘It started off easy.’

The initial wave of undead consisted of just zombies and skeletons.

With modern firepower, this was a manageable force.

But, after a few repulsions, the Undead Legion that appeared were entirely different.

‘The intangible enemy, ghosts, couldn’t be combated with just an army.’

North Korea’s fall was mainly due to these intangible monsters.

Of course, North Korea’s downfall was partly because they nationally suppressed the growth of players and were caught off guard.

Other countries tried to counter the intangible monsters by boosting the power of Mage and Support players.

In the beginning, this approach had some success, but

‘They were outnumbered.’

Once a dungeon portal opened, undead poured out without pause.

Initially, the dungeon portals released not just undead but various monsters of different types.

But at some point, only ghost legions holding the flag of ‘Kill the King’ aggressively emerged.

‘Eventually, every region had the flag of ‘Kill the King’ planted…’

Once dungeons began to emerge, it was the end for that country.

Such a perception existed even during this tutorial phase, and it was solidified mainly due to the ghost legions of Kill the King.

‘Facing ‘Kill the King’ won’t make this game easy.’

Seong Jihan looked around after being summoned.

The direction he had to defend was the northern area.

“Not inside the tomb, but outside.”

Sturdy walls encircled the tomb in all four cardinal directions.

Seong Jihan stood on the northern wall, where guards made of clay, like those he had seen in old dungeon maps, were positioned at various points.

[Player ‘Seong Jihan’ and 9 lower-ranked players will defend the north, while the remaining 90 players will defend the south.]

[The side whose gate breaks first will be defeated and ranked at the very bottom.]

One by one, party members were summoned to the northern gate.

Just like the system message indicated, only nine more were summoned, and no additional personnel were placed.

The intention to suppress the game-breaker Seong Jihan was evident.

[You have completed the achievement ‘Becoming a Balance Adjuster(1)’.]

[You have earned 30,000 achievement points.]

“There was an achievement for this?”

After checking the achievement message, Seong Jihan looked at his teammates.

“Ah… To be on the same team as Seong Jihan…”

“Sigh… We’ve lost…”

Players from around the world who were summoned looked at Seong Jihan, clearly showing their disappointment.

Whether you win or lose in the Top 100 match, you still get promoted just by playing.

But everyone wanted to be on the winning side, not the losing one.

“Am I really ranked below 90? That’s impossible…!”

Being summoned here was essentially a system confirmation that they were below the top 90 of the Top 100.

No one was in high spirits.

“Oh! Jihan! Hey! We’re on the same side!”

Supporter Sophia, seeing Seong Jihan, jumped around with excitement.

“Sophia? How come you’re here…?”

Seong Jihan blinked in surprise.

She shouldn’t be ranked here, should she?

To his question, Sophia smiled brightly.

“Oh, I didn’t use my stat points.”

‘Is it because of the Otherworldly Relic?’

An Otherworldly Relic raises divine power if you hold onto it and pray.

The one who told him about this was none other than the future Sophia.

Seong Jihan roughly guessed why she didn’t allocate her stats.

“Don’t you wonder why?”

As she approached with sparkling eyes, she prompted him to ask the question he wanted.

“I am curious.”

“Heh. Right? You’re curious, aren’t you? Then treat me to a meal, and I’ll tell you!”

“I don’t have time to go to America.”

“I’ll go to Korea! It’s perfect timing since there’s a break after the promotion match!”

…She’s coming to Korea just for a meal?

Seong Jihan was flabbergasted.

‘When did she start having a thing for me?’

In his previous life, when he was part of America First, Sophia had shown interest in Seong Jihan.

Back then, Seong Jihan, preoccupied with other matters, had ignored her.

But Sophia at that time was quite serious.

‘I had a rough idea why back then…’

In the game, and even in reality, he had rescued her multiple times, shielding her from enemies and becoming a reliable protector.

Even if it wasn’t intentional, Seong Jihan had collaborated with Sophia in key moments as teammates.

Perhaps that’s why, aside from Sophia, many Supporter and Mages from America First had a favorable impression of him.

If it wasn’t for Seong Jihan, dozens would have died in real battles.

‘But why is she behaving this way now?’

Looking at Sophia waiting for his response, Seong Jihan frowned, unable to understand.

The mystery remained unsolved until he found out that she had a severe warrior mania.

And all these scenes of Sophia approaching Seong Jihan were being broadcasted live worldwide.

A fierce long-range firepower battle unfolded between the two teams.

Southgate Map.

There are typically two ways to win on this map. The first is a showdown between long-range positions to determine the outcome.

The attacking team’s warriors focus on protecting their own long-range dealers, rather than entering the fortress. They exchange long-range attacks, and the number of player deaths is counted. In such battles, the main actors are the long-range dealers. This situation occurred when the warrior levels on both sides were similar.

However, Russia believed that their warriors would always win when they clashed in combat.

The second winning formula is to break through the warriors guarding the fortress. This was a fairly risky tactic. If the number of warriors was the same on both sides, and the warriors guarding the fortress received support from the wall above, it was difficult for the attacking team’s warriors to match the firepower.

The warriors of the attacking team would be vulnerable if the warriors guarding the fortress held out a little longer. As a result, unless there was a significant difference in warrior level, most teams preferred the first winning formula.

Russia’s charge implied that the Korean national team’s warriors were of a lower caliber. That viewpoint was shared by Ha Yeonjoo.

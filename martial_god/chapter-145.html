The Guild Master’s Office at the Daegi Guild.

Lee Hayeon reported to Seong Jihan with a serious expression, “Boss, the Chinese players in our guild are suddenly terminating their lease contracts.”

Seong Jihan asked, “How many are there?”

“Because we had some new recruits this time… it’s about six of them.”

Due to the unique effect of the Phoenix Flag, the guild’s capacity had increased to 30 members. With six Chinese players among them, it accounted for about 20 percent.

“Quite a few Chinese players, huh.” Seong Jihan said with a perplexed expression.

“With the recent open positions, most of them were won in auctions. They are each being paid around 10 billion won per month.”

“So, every month, we are losing 60 billion won?”

“Wang Lin joined us for 5 billion won. So, it’s exactly 55 billion won.”

“Well, we’ll send them off and find replacements.”

“If we recruit new members… it might reduce our profits compared to last time. Are you okay with that?”

When Lee Hayeon cautiously raised this concern, Seong Jihan nodded confidently.

“Yes, it’s okay. No need to worry.”

Seong Jihan smiled.

Even if they suffered a little financial loss, Seong Jihan wasn’t the kind of person to blink an eye to monetary loss.

“Please inform the People’s Association right away about our intention to cancel the contracts.”

“Of course, I understand.”

Lee Hayeon made a phone call on the spot to inform the People’s Association of the contract cancellations. Afterward, she chatted with Seong Jihan.

Observing her like this, it seemed she wasn’t too concerned about the situation.

“Our chat manager suddenly has a lot of work to do. He’s been overwhelmed with the workload. The hate comments are suddenly pouring into the guild channel, not just Seong Jihan’s channel.”

“They are persistent.” Seong Jihan grinned.

“Yes, they are. Is it really that important to find out the name of a martial art?”

She shook her head in confusion. Then, she sipped her coffee and suddenly her eyes widened when she saw a message on her tablet.

“Huh? Wang Lin wants to talk to you. He really wants to have a conversation with you, sir.”

“Is that so?”

“If you’re going to have a conversation, I can be the interpreter in the middle.”

“…Can you speak Chinese?”

“Yes, I’ve studied it.”

She was quite talented. He wondered why she only gambled so much in her previous life.

“Then, please.”

“We did what you guys wanted.”

[Well, I actually want to stay in the Daegi Guild… But according to the government’s policy, I had no choice…]

Wang Lin subtly brought up the main point.

[In life, being too stubborn often leads to more losses than gains, don’t you think? Right now, the government is quite angry, saying that Seong Jihan won’t yield at all. If we go all the way here, it will only harm you. I can mediate to make sure things are resolved smoothly.]

Three days before the match against China, at the BattleNet Center’s operations room, the South Korean national team coach, Noh Youngjun, furrowed his brow deeply.

“China… They’re just too strong…”

If Russia had the most powerful Warriors, China boasted the world’s top players in every class except Warriors. In particular, China had SSS-level gift users among their mages and supporters. Even during the reign of the Sword King, South Korea’s win rate in the Korea vs. China matches couldn’t surpass 40 percent.

“So, in the end, I had no choice but to pick these guys…”

Noh Youngjun glanced at the player roster. The lineup for the Korea vs. China match included first-team Warriors who had been cut from the national team roster in the previous selection, except for the player who assaulted Ha Yoori. The pressure from their respective top-ten guilds to bring back these players played a part in their selection, but it was Coach Noh Youngjun’s determination to somehow counter China that was the most significant factor.

He hoped that by creating synergy with Seong Jihan, there might be a slight increase in the chances of winning, even if it was still close to zero. Netizens’ reactions aligned with Coach Noh Youngjun’s expectations.

–Ugh, Do we really need those trashes of society?.

–Isn’t this too lenient of a punishment?

–Well, at least they didn’t call the guy who assaulted Ha Yoori. Having them might give us a fighting chance against China, don’t you think?

–I guess.

This was because China’s intense pressure to reveal Seong Jihan’s gift had offended Korean pride.

They must win against those Chinese guys this time, even if they have to call these trashes to society.

–For real, did you see what they were doing to Seong Jihan? They’re making a big fuss trying to find out his gift, and it’s so ridiculous, ㅋㅋㅋ.

–Well, if it’s a Chinese thing, what can you do?

‘Hmm. It’s good to be back.’

“Like they say, the Center has a good smell!”

Lee Yoongi, who always accompanied him, followed with a grin.

“Just because the public backlash is less due to China. Don’t you dare cause any trouble again!” Coach told them off angrily.

“Haha, got it. Coach, Is Seong Jihan not here?”

“Why?”

“I want to apologize for the last incident.” Kim Dongwoo said with a slight wry smile.

Coach Noh Youngjun blinked his eyes.

He was chased out and now he wanted to apologize? Was he really going to apologize?

Spotted: Seong Jihan’s Girlfriend!

The photo boldly captured the moment just before they kissed.

“Th-That girl… isn’t she the Guild Master of the Divergent Guild?”

A face that anyone in the industry would recognize.

Coach Noh Youngjun immediately identified the woman.

“Seong Jihan doesn’t have a way out. He got caught by patch kids.”

“To be honest, I found that woman suspicious when she became a guild master. She wasn’t even a player, but she became a guild master, right?”

“She’s pretty, though. She didn’t come out even when they called celebrities. There’s a reason she’s from the Lee family of Lee Sangjin.”

The two Warriors had no shortage of criticism for Lee Hayeon, but Coach Noh Youngjun’s expression hardened as he read the article.

“Hmm… I wonder if Seong Jihan can handle this.”

With all the issues surrounding China and now even a dating scandal, Seong Jihan’s mental state might be too shaky, right?

The coach was worried about Seong Jihan, and that’s why he called him to the BattleNet Center immediately upon his arrival.

“Oh. I actually set that up.”

Seong Jihan, who was called in, seemed utterly unfazed.

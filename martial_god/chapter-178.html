[Although I’m Brahma’s representative, I can’t completely go against the will of the 77,000 Thunder gods. And, every Thunder god was one of the most powerful gods in their own worlds. To them, avoiding a challenger is unimaginable.]

Many Thunder gods had reigned as god-kings in various mythologies.

To them, a challenger was something to naturally be trampled and driven off.

Abandoning the throne and fleeing from the enemy went against the Thunder god’s pride.

So most of the Thunder gods readily decided to face the Wandering Martial God.

“You’re a Thunder god too, how did you think to flee then?”

‘The Martial God is that powerful…’ He expected The Martial God would be strong.

That’s why he had the reputation of a constellation-hunting Constellation.

But to blanket Myriad Ghost Sealing Gods over an entire planet?

Having personally used it himself, Seong Jihan knew how preposterous that was.

‘Even using it as a skill in my past life, a few meters was the limit…’

Of course, he could expand the range much further now with Spirit Embodiment.

But still, to blanket Myriad Ghost Sealing Gods over a whole planet?

It was unthinkable no matter how he thought about it.

[That being… is an unstoppable, disastrous existence. I persuaded the other Brahmas as hard as I could, but they wouldn’t listen. Rather, they threatened to pull me from my position of representative if I kept saying such things.]

“So you want to escape.”

The Crimson Thunder Cannon was that amazing?

It was just mixing the Phoenix’s fire powers and the Divine thunder…

Seong Jihan couldn’t understand why the Brahma leader was overvaluing his Crimson Thunder Cannon so much, but

‘Well, no harm in accepting if he’s willing to pay a lot.’

He was determined to squeeze out as much compensation as possible from this blue lion bearing his abdomen.

“Then I’ll do everything reasonable.”

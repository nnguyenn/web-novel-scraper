[Welcome to the Bronze League – Gangnam Area]

[The mission is Defense.]

Seong Jihan, after returning home, logged into the game while wearing a suit.

“It’s a zombie mission this time.”

The mission was to survive while blocking the zombies coming up the tower until only five teams remained.

The achievement quest window still displayed the uncompleted chain quest from last time.

[Chain Quest – Zombie’s Source (1)]

* Eliminate 1,000 zombies alone.

[Reward – Achievement Points 3,000]

* Upon completion, the quest continues to Zombie’s Source (2).

“At that time, it was impossible, but now I can easily clear it.” During the previous game, Seong Jihan had low levels and power, but now his abilities had greatly improved, and he had gained another unique stat boost.

With this much strength, he could easily sweep away the zombies below the tower.

He had also prepared items that were perfect for this map.

“Inventory.”

Seong Jihan opened his inventory and summoned the weapons within it.

In his right hand, a large sword the size of a person appeared.

[Otherworldly Artifact – Urk’s Divine Executioner Sword (C-Rank)]

– A sword used by Urk’s high priest to offer sacrifices at the altar of the gods.

– Excellent for cleaving through monsters’ bodies.

When his divine power reached 10.

[From now on, you can only absorb Divine Power from relics that are C-rank or higher.]

Receiving this message, he purchased the item for testing purposes.

‘I wonder if absorbing divine power from artifacts of the Otherworld will also increase my force.’

A unique stat boost that combined divine power and mana.

To see if absorbing divine power would increase his force, Seong Jihan boldly spent all his GP to purchase a C-Rank weapon.

‘It’s quite big.’

Despite being C-Rank, the price was not as high as he expected.

Urk’s Divine Executioner Sword was too large and heavy for humans to wield comfortably.

A sword that many warrior classes had avoided for that reason.

Swish! Swish!

“It’s usable.”

However, when such a weapon came into Seong Jihan’s hands, it spun as lightly as a wooden stick.

The synergy between power and force made it even more powerful, enabling this.

“Now that I’m all prepared, I want to go down right away, but…”

The defense map wouldn’t start until all the team members were summoned.

Seong Jihan turned on the Battle Tube broadcast while waiting for the rest of the team to join.

– Oh? Seong Jihan has started the game.

Perhaps it hadn’t been long since the Sword King’s auction had ended. It hadn’t been that long since the broadcast had started, but more people were joining than expected.

Since the game hadn’t started, the chat window was open and active.

Most viewers were curious about the ability Seong Jihan had demonstrated.

Even though it was just a performance of tearing a business card, it was an out-of-spec power within the Battle Net class.

Not only regular viewers but also the scouts were paying close attention to Seong Jihan.

“I guess it’s time to make a bet.”

Seong Jihan checked the channel’s subscribers.

Over 12,000.

Perhaps due to the exclusive coverage of the auction, there were more than 4,000 additional subscribers compared to before.

As a result, one achievement quest had been cleared.

[The channel’s subscribers have exceeded 10,000.]

[Cleared the regular achievement, ‘Recruit Subscribers (1).’]

[Reward: 1,000 Achievement Points.]

‘From now on, increasing subscribers won’t be easy.’

Up until now, he had been at the center of events that attracted the world’s attention, so his subscribers had rapidly increased as a secondary effect.

Of course, Seong Jihan’s potential was exceptional, so he could easily increase subscribers by moving to a higher league, but…

‘Instead of waiting idly for that, I should make the most of what I have here.’

Seong Jihan decided to take advantage of the current situation where aggro was drawn to him.

“Hello, everyone.”

Was Battle Net upholding justice by breaking through Seong Jihan’s arrogant declaration?

The members summoned to be on Seong Jihan’s team were quite remarkable.

The so-called top three from Gangnam’s Bronze League.

The renowned man, Streamer Golden Light.

The magic user with a fluctuating strength, Kim Gyuhyuk.

And…

– The God of Soccer.

– Diego Masid!

– The archer who shoots soccer balls instead of arrows!

Diego Masid was an Archer who was considered the worst team player.

Despite being a long-range dealer, he never dealt any damage and only threw soccer balls, making him a legendary troller.

His playing pattern was simple. He throws soccer balls from his inventory of 10 slots, and once he uses up all 10 soccer balls, he stands still until he gets defeated.

In a different Bronze League, people might consider this a comedic act, or it might pass as entertainment. But in the most serious and intense Gangnam Area, the situation is different.

Teaming up with players like him in such a serious and competitive environment made the others want to tear their hair out.

However…

Upon seeing Diego Masid’s name in the chat, Seong Jihan’s eyes widened.

‘Masid?’

Diego Masid, Argentina’s top player. He was a powerful player who had upheld the ranking of Argentina’s league all by himself. While he didn’t make it to the final ten countries and eventually fell in battle. Still, his unique playstyle garnered him high fame even after his fall.

“But… he was a Mage class?”

Remembering the videos of Masid he had seen before, Seong Jihan smirked. His appearance was vastly different from the clean look in the videos. His face was untidy due to the long beard, and his body was lean and emaciated.

As Seong Jihan silently gazed at Masid, Masid lifted his head and said in broken Korean, ” Don’t mind me.”

After stumbling through some Korean words, Masid kicked the ball again. It must be at this point that he earns the title of a true troll.

‘But… he doesn’t seem interested in communication.’

There was no need to waste time on a partner who clearly had no intention of conversing. There was no need to pour time into someone who seemed unwilling to engage in a conversation.

Seong Jihan turned his head, deciding to gather information about him later.

“Seong Jihan-nim! I can’t believe we’re meeting like this! Nice to meet you!”

Streamer Golden Light approached Seong Jihan in an excited state.

“….”

Mage Kim Gyuhyuk stood there with an expression that remained as distrustful as ever.

‘To think I’d end up matching with this guy again…’

He wanted to give him advice as his senior, but that arrogant Jihan had thrown him off of the tower, and remembering that made him want to attack right away.

‘One doesn’t avoid feces because it’s scary but because it’s dirty…’ Gyuhyuk mentally claimed victory and decided to hold back. After all, if he picked up on him, he would die again.

“Everyone, today’s broadcast is amazing. Not only are we playing a team game with the talk of the town, Seong Jihan-nim, but also… Oh my, to have all three of Gangnam’s top players in one place!”

“Hah, the top three of Gangnam. Who came up with that? I want to sue whoever coined that phrase.”

“Haha, that’s true. Oh, but Kim Gyuhyuk-nim… you’re a Mage, right?”

Kim Gyuhyuk gave Streamer Golden Light a side glance.

Streamer Golden Light was decked out in A-rank equipment. He was not someone to be taken lightly.

Out of his mouth, polite language naturally flowed.

“…Indeed.”

“That’s strange. I was sure Seong Jihan-nim was a Mage class… because he used Fire earlier in the broadcast.”

“That Fuc… No, that person… When he was summoned with me, he appeared as a Support.”

“Wow! Really?? Seong Jihan-nim, what in the world… Could you give a satisfying answer for the viewers?”

Streamer Golden Light looked at Seong Jihan with sparkling eyes.

Expecting an answer, his gaze was filled with anticipation.

Seong Jihan brushed it off nonchalantly.

“That might be difficult. After all, I made a bet on my channel.”

“Come on, don’t be like that!”

“But more importantly.”

“I’ll be heading down to the bottom, so I’ll leave the top to you guys.”

‘I didn’t get any achievement points.’

Perhaps because he had already achieved the 10-kill achievement in the previous survival map, this game only had level-ups and GP gains, but no achievement point gains.

Still, Seong Jihan decided not to be disappointed anymore.

‘Things are different now.’

[Reaching level 15 has unlocked the game map ‘Dungeon’.]

Dungeon.

Unlike Survival and Defense modes, which are available from the first login to BattleNet, the Dungeon was the third game map, unlocked at level 15.

‘This is where the real team play starts.’

Dungeons weren’t content you could clear just by being strong.

With various traps, mazes, and puzzles, players needed party members who worked well together.

Perhaps that’s why there was another feature unlocked at level 15.

[Guild content has been unlocked.]

[You can now engage in party play with guild members.]

From now on, he could form a team with guild members and play the game on any party play map.

‘From now on, a guild is almost essential.’

Between a party formed by guild members and a public party mixed with regular players, it was easy to predict which would perform better.

For instance, if a troll backed by the Sword King Fan Club, like last time, appeared, they might not even complete the mission.

Even so, Seong Jihan had no intention of joining a guild yet.

‘I need to achieve the Guild Master achievement.’

BattleNet achievement points, which could be harvested in various ways outside the game.

Up until now, he had acquired many from BattleTube, but after that, the place to get achievement points was related to guild achievements.

“But I have to reach Silver rank to create a guild, so until then, I can’t join any guild.”

Joining a guild would be beneficial now.

Given Seong Jihan’s skills and potential, astronomical signing bonuses, salaries, and the best equipment would be provided.

But those would come naturally once he possessed the capability.

He had no intention of giving up the Guild Master achievement, aimed firmly at being number one.

‘Anyway, I need to go to the dungeon without a guild for now.’

Seong Jihan searched for August’s Bronze Dungeon.

If August’s Bronze Dungeon mainly had traps and mazes, he could simply overpower it. But if there were patterns requiring party cooperation, he needed a strategy.

[August’s Bronze Dungeon]

– Conqueror’s Mausoleum

‘Conqueror’s Mausoleum? Sounds vaguely familiar.’

He couldn’t remember this dungeon well. Being a world-class player, remembering a Bronze Dungeon from 10 years ago was unlikely.

Seong Jihan skimmed through the strategy guide with a wry smile.

[Conqueror’s Mausoleum is a dungeon where powerful monsters appear, making it relatively difficult.]

[The role of a Warrior leading the front line is crucial.]

[There are few traps and mazes, and hardly any puzzles, but all party members must be alive in front of the Conqueror’s statue on the 7th basement floor.]

Seong Jihan’s expression stiffened.

All four party members had to survive to clear the dungeon.

“With these conditions….”

If interference from the Sword King Fan Club continued like before, the issue wouldn’t be about ranking first, but potentially failing the mission entirely.

After pondering for a moment, Seong Jihan soon broke into a faint smile.

‘There’s a way, but only I can do it.’

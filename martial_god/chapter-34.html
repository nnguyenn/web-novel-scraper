Chapter 34

* * *

Black Eagle Guild.

While it didn’t rank among the top 10 guilds, it was a renowned guild that sat comfortably among the high ranks.

The Black Eagle Guild members, who had entered the Gangnam 1st Division, were the rising stars, aiming to potentially become professionals in the future.

However, even for them…

“A million GP…”

“Ten…billion?”

10 billion was an astronomical sum.

“You can’t play around with viewer missions. If you don’t have money, you can’t set such stakes. 10 billion… is it for real?”

Gulp.

The warrior involuntarily swallowed.

10 billion.

Even if he was considered a promising talent within the guild, for someone under a bronze contract like him, this was an unimaginable amount.

Perhaps, if he climbed up to the gold league, that amount might feel attainable.

‘But… getting to gold isn’t easy.’ the Warrior thought.

If he had a top rank Gift, he would have joined a top 10 guild, not the slightly inferior Black Eagle Guild.

He considered himself talented but an average amongst the elites.

Could he, having possibly passed the silver promotion match, overcome the barrier to gold?

Never.

“If I commit suicide now, my reputation might take a hit, but…”

10 billion was an enticing figure.

“For all I know, the hit to my reputation might not be as bad as I think.”

Having watched Seong Jihan’s channel a few times, the warrior was familiar with the channel’s dynamics.

There were probably quite a few people hoping that Seong Jihan wouldn’t secure the top spot so they could see his status window.

The more spectacularly Seong Jihan displayed his powers, the more viewers grew curious about his abilities.

So, even if he commits suicide here, he might just get some flak, and it would die down.

“Yeah, let’s do it!”

The warrior looked around.

He noticed his fellow guild members acting suspiciously.

The archer took out an arrow and discreetly moved it toward his own throat.

The mage, despite no monsters being around, seemed to be preparing a spell.

The warrior felt a rush of urgency.

“Did those fools… also get missions from the Sword King?”

Those crazy bastards!

Did all three of them really get a 10 billion GP mission?

Then, if they all die, who gets the money?

“…I don’t know how it will turn out, but I need to die first.”

He was determined, and turned the tip of his sword towards his own throat.

He had never imagined he would consider suicide in BattleNet, but he needed to quickly clear the mission.

Swish!

“Huh…?”

Just as the sword was about to pierce his throat, it suddenly halted.

“Why… can’t I move?”

Only a slight push was needed to end it, but his entire body felt restrained by an unknown force.

And then…

“As expected.”

Seong Jihan, who was walking ahead, had turned around, a cold smile playing on his lips.

Thud! Thud!

The sound of footsteps thundered in his ears like a storm.

The warrior’s eyes darted around.

Not just him, all the guild members seemed paralyzed, unable to move.

“Telekinesis…! Did he anticipate something like this?”

Thinking back, something had been bothering him.

Given Seong Jihan’s power, he could have simply progressed through the game alone, clearing the path ahead. Yet, he had always ensured he didn’t stray too far from the party members.

Even when confronting enemies, he always maintained a certain distance.

At that time, they had assumed he was doing so to protect them…

‘He was monitoring us!’

This realization sent shivers down his spine.

“If you were going to do it, you should have done it earlier.”

Tap! Tap! Tap!

Seong Jihan’s fingers lightly tapped various points on the warrior’s body.

His sturdy armor felt like it was being pierced with ease.

“Wha…”

Suddenly, the warrior lost control of his body.

No. To be precise, he could only move his face and neck.

“My arms and legs…”

It was a completely different sensation from earlier.

Before, he had felt forcefully restrained by a powerful external force, but now his body simply wouldn’t obey him.

“This… this is!”

The warrior suddenly recalled the martial arts techniques used by the higher job class ‘Martial Artist’ for warriors.

“Could it be… Acupoint Sealing?”

Clang!

His sword and shield dropped uselessly as the warrior collapsed, drained of strength.

It wasn’t just the warrior.

“Ah…!”

“Why… why are you doing this?!”

Turning his neck with great effort to look behind, he saw the other two guild members collapsed, limp as if their strings had been cut.

Seong Jihan, looking down at them with an icy gaze, coldly remarked, “Was 10 billion that desirable to you?”

“How… how did you know about it?”

The warrior asked, surprised.

Even if he had anticipated it, how could he specify the exact amount?

“I had someone inform me.”

Seong Jihan’s gaze turned to the void.

Specifically,

[Streamer Golden Light has donated 10,000GP.]

[Be careful, Seong Jihan! Those guild members want to commit suicide for 10 billion!]

He looked at the donation message that had popped up in his view.

* * *

Just moments ago.

-“I want to be your editor, Jihan! Please let me!”

-“No thanks.”

After being rejected so quickly by Seong Jihan,

“Sigh…”

Streamer Golden Light was feeling downcast after skipping his streams for two days.

“If I had collaborated with Jihan, we could have become legends on BattleTube… To let such an opportunity slip away…”

He had a dream.

To be the very best on BattleTube.

Though he was currently trolling and doing deep-sea exploration content. One day, he vowed to be the top broadcaster!

That was Streamer Golden Light’s grand ambition.

“But it’s too much for me alone.”

Streamer Golden Light was well aware of his own limitations.

Though he had good looks and an A-grade Gift, it wasn’t enough to be the best on BattleTube.

Only top-tier players like Sword King could reign supreme on BattleTube.

“I saw that potential in Seong Jihan… To let it slip away like this…”

It was depressing.

‘So depressed!’

Just as Streamer Golden Light was wallowing in his self-pity,

Ding!

[Seong Jihan has started streaming.]

His phone buzzed with a notification.

“Huh? At this hour?”

Streamer Golden Light looked at the clock in surprise.

Early morning.

It was the time when viewership was at its lowest as everyone would be heading to work or school.

Who streams at such a crappy time?

“This is why I need to be there to advise him!”

With a sigh of lament, Streamer Golden Light accessed Seong Jihan’s channel.

Seong Jihan, now at Level 15 and navigating the dungeon map, was clearing the dungeon with his usual overwhelming power.

The game was progressing smoothly.

“With this pace, he’ll definitely rank first. If only there aren’t any issues…”

[Immediately commit in-game suicide to hinder Seong Jihan from achieving 1st place.]

50 million won.

Even if his loyalty toward the Sword King was exceptional, it wasn’t something easily understandable.

“Actually, the person who made me curse at Sword King seemed more like a fan.”

However, a single case was not enough to draw conclusions.

But the 10 billion figure, informed by Streamer Golden Light, crossed the line.

“Doesn’t it seem strange to all of you?”

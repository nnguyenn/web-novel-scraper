Chapter 429

On the wall of the Golden Tower.

“Ugh… ugh…”

There, Gilgamesh with a haggard face was only groaning with unfocused eyes.

“Why is he like this?”

“Ah, we’re trying to plant him as a human world tree.”

To Ashoka’s question, Pythia answered with a beaming smile.

“As a world tree?”

“Yeah. You know well how we handled him before, right?”

The ‘before’ she was talking about must mean before the infinite regression.

Ashoka shook his head.

“I was only at the seal site, so I don’t know what happened to him.”

“Ah, is that so? You didn’t do any external activities. I didn’t know. Since I’m not in a position of constantly regressing like you.”

“For that, you seem to know well about the past.”

“After learning about my past, the performance of Divine Eye has strengthened.”

Tap. Tap.

Pythia, tapping her forehead, placed her hand on the golden wall.

Then,

“Aaaaargh!”

Gilgamesh’s face contorted greatly as he screamed from pain.

Swish…

“It’s a world tree fruit. Since it’s a simple version, the performance is weaker than the original, but it will help with wound recovery.”

“Thanks.”

Ashoka, receiving the golden apple, glanced at Gilgamesh who was suffering beside him.

“I’m grateful to you too.”

While saying that, Ashoka tapped his head.

Gilgamesh, who had been suffering, immediately widened his eyes at this treatment.

“You, you bastard… Are you insulting me?! No matter how I look like this now, I am your ancestor…!”

“I just said thank you. Ancestor.”

“Why are you mixing words like that?”

Bam!

Beside them, Pythia slapped Gilgamesh’s head as if it was good.

“You can just beat him.”

“You, ugh…”

“Looks like you’ve been quite violent. Seeing how he’s become quiet.”

“Hmph. Well, I did it moderately. These days are the most enjoyable time of my life.”

“Is that so? Anyway, I’ll eat this well.”

Crunch!

As Ashoka ate the golden apple, the wound on his chest quickly began to heal.

As the energy of the void began to dissipate, Pythia quietly looked at him.

“Ashoka, why do you help our Lord?”

“Suddenly asking that between fellow disciples?”

“Just. I don’t understand why someone like you would do this. You were once almost a saint of humanity, right? I’m wondering why such a noble person is helping with the massacre of humanity.”

Pythia was smiling brightly, but she was looking at Ashoka with a calmly examining gaze.

“I have a goal.”

“What goal?”

“To erase the red fire inherent in humanity.”

“Aha…”

“And this can only be solved by Martial God.”

“Turns out we had the same goal?”

Pythia nodded upon hearing those words.

“Was this your goal too? Not revenge?”

“This is revenge. Locking me in a lab and turning me into a baby-making machine… Why do you think they did that? It was to put the Red Administrator into humanity and revive him. So I have to interfere with that purpose.”

“I see… So the destruction of humanity wasn’t the purpose.”

“They are still my descendants after all. It’s inevitable that they keep getting destroyed in the infinite regression… but when Lord Martial God achieves his goal, they will be saved in the end. I’m just waiting for that time.”

Pythia had such an intention?

As Ashoka was calmly examining her with sunken eyes,

“Hah. Trusting a snake? Utterly foolish…! Can’t you see my state and still not know?”

Gilgamesh finally raised his face again and sneered at them.

“He even tricked me with the contract. Why would you be any different? Why would he bother erasing the red fire engraved in humanity? He would just burn them all!”

“You still have energy left, huh? Guess I didn’t beat you enough.”

“Kuk kuk… Utterly foolish. Trusting a snake’s promise… You will soon end up like me too. I’ll be waiting for you to join me…”

As Gilgamesh was sneering like that,

Bam!

Before Pythia could even raise her hand first, Ashoka struck his head hard.

“Don’t worry. I’m more thorough with contracts than you.”

“Ugh… ack…!”

“Then, I’ll go recover.”

“Okay. I’ll be planting the tree~”

Pythia smiled as if she liked Ashoka hitting Gilgamesh, seeing him off.

Around the time he disappeared like that,

‘…What?’

Gilgamesh, who had been bound to the tower, gritted his teeth and looked at Ashoka, feeling his body becoming a bit more free.

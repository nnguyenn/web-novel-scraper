[You’ve turned 18 years old and awakened as a player.]

“Phew…”

Yoon Seah sighed unknowingly.

Thankfully, she had obtained a Gift.

Even for students of the academy, the chance of getting a Gift was not more than 50%.


More than half of the awakened players didn’t have a Gift, so she was off to a good start.

“…Status Window.”

Yoon Seah opened her status window and her eyes widened.

The very first stats shown were Strength, Agility, Stamina, and Magic Power – all of them were recorded as 10.

They were maxed-out stats!

Yoon Seah felt a surge of pride without realizing it.

But that joy was short-lived.

The most crucial part was yet to come – the slot for the Gift at the very bottom of the status window.

Of course, the initial stats were an essential factor in determining a player’s talent.


However, the grade of the Gift was even more vital in determining whether one would remain an amateur or turn professional.

“…Huh?”

And then,

Yoon Seah’s eyes widened once more as she saw her Gift.

[Gift – Late Bloomer (Rank F)]

“F…”

Rank F for the Gift.

No matter how random Gifts were…She never expected to receive such a rank.

She slumped down, feeling as if she couldn’t stand.

Even after awakening as a player and feeling healthier than before, she felt paralyzed.

…It was cold.

Despite being summer in August. She felt a chill throughout her body.

She couldn’t believe what she was seeing.

Hoping it was just a dream, she closed and opened her eyes repeatedly.

However, the Status Window was ruthless.

[Gift – Late Bloomer (Rank F)

Her dreams had shattered.

It felt as if the whole world was crumbling around her.

What should she do now?

She hadn’t considered the possibility of receiving an F-rank gift.

‘I can’t become a player now.’

With an F and an ambiguous Gift called “Late Bloomer”, the prospects of becoming a BattleNet player weren’t bright.

‘Should I prepare for college entrance exams?’

At least she had maintained good grades, so as not to tarnish the name of the Sword King.

She sighed again.

She just wanted to sit down and do nothing.

‘If it was going to turn out like this, why did the Gift Gem shine so brightly?…’

The Gift Gem, which shone even more brilliantly than that of a senior who received an S grade, had falsely raised her hopes.

She almost shed tears thinking about how hopeful she had been just moments ago.

“…I won’t cry.”

After taking a deep breath, Yoon Seah stood up.

What’s done was done.

Her uncle would be waiting outside, so she couldn’t stay slumped here forever.

‘Come to think of it… did Uncle know something?’

As Yoon Seah left the Gift Hall, she recalled how Seong Jihan had consistently emphasised that ‘Gifts are random’ recently.

He had stressed the randomness to such an extent that at times, it felt somewhat hurtful.

But after receiving an F, she felt grateful for his words.

If she hadn’t been prepared by his talks, her disappointment would have been even more profound.

‘First… let’s get out quickly. And… I should thank Uncle.’

Step by step, Yoon Seah tried to compose herself, rubbing her eyes as she slowly exited the Gift Hall.

Whoosh!

“…”

She saw something floating in mid-air and upon closer inspection,

“…Is that, hair?”

Short strands of sandy-yellow hair which was clumped together with wax was floating in the air as if it were a clump of weeds that had been plucked out.

Beyond the man’s hair, which looked like it was styled after a Japanese samurai was…

“It works, huh?”

Her Uncle, with one corner of his mouth raised, was making a gesture with his fingers.
